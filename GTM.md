# GTM.md — Go-to-Market Strategy

## Exact Target User

**Primary:** Engineering Manager or VP Engineering at a Series A–B SaaS startup (15–100 employees). They manage a team of 6–25 developers. They signed up for AI tools ad-hoc over 12 months as tools launched — first GitHub Copilot, then Cursor for some devs, then someone expensed ChatGPT Team, then Claude showed up on the credit card. They have no single view of total AI spend. They know they're paying for multiple tools but don't know if the stack is redundant or optimal.

**Secondary:** CTO or Founder at a pre-Series A company (5–20 people). They're cost-conscious. They personally approved every tool subscription but haven't re-evaluated since initial signup.

**Not the target (right now):** Individual developers, Fortune 500 companies (procurement processes too slow), or non-technical teams (they don't have the stack complexity that generates savings).

## First 100 Users in 30 Days — $0 Budget

**Week 1 — Warm channels:**
- Post to personal Twitter/X with a real audit example: "I audited our 12-person team's AI spend. Found $340/mo in waste. Here's what I found →" with a screenshot of SpendLens results. No product pitch, just the result.
- Submit to Hacker News as Show HN: "Show HN: SpendLens – Free AI spend audit for startups." Post at 9am Eastern on a Tuesday. Be in the comments all day.
- Post in Indie Hackers "Show Your Project" section with the HN post linked.

**Week 2 — DM campaigns:**
- Identify 50 engineering managers on X who have recently tweeted about AI tools or Cursor/Copilot. DM them with: "Hey — built a tool that audits AI tool spend. Ran it on our stack and found $X/mo waste. Happy to run it on yours for free — takes 2 min." Personal, not spammy. No pitch deck.
- Find the top 3 threads in r/ExperiencedDevs about AI coding tools. Comment with a genuinely useful breakdown of the findings, mention the tool at the end.

**Week 3 — Content seeding:**
- Write a Medium post: "We audited our AI tool spend and found $X/month in redundancy. Here's what we changed." Real numbers from the SpendLens audit. Link to the tool at the end.
- Submit to TLDR newsletter as a tool recommendation (free submissions).
- Share in Rands Leadership Slack's #tools channel.

**Week 4 — Referral nudge:**
- Email the users who captured their report and ask: "Did the audit surface anything surprising? We'd love a quote for our site — and if you share it with a team that runs it, we'll send you a $25 gift card."
